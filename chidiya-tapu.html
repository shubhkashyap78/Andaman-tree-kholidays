Port Blair, Andaman

About Chidiya Tapu

Chidiya Tapu, meaning "Bird Island" in Hindi, is located at the southernmost tip of South Andaman, about 25 km from Port Blair. This picturesque destination is renowned for its rich biodiversity, lush green forests, and spectacular sunset views. The area is a haven for bird watchers with over 46 species of birds including endemic species like Andaman Woodpecker, Andaman Drongo, and Andaman Treepie. The beach offers stunning views of the sunset over the Bay of Bengal, making it one of the most romantic spots in Andaman. The Biological Park nearby houses various species of birds, animals, and reptiles native to the islands.

Things to Do at Chidiya Tapu

Bird Watching

Spot endemic bird species like Andaman Woodpecker, Andaman Drongo, White-headed Starling, and Andaman Treepie. Best time: Early morning (6:00 AM - 9:00 AM).

Sunset Viewing

Watch the spectacular sunset from the sunset point. The sun setting over the Bay of Bengal creates a mesmerizing view. Arrive by 4:30 PM to get a good spot.

Nature Trekking

Explore the Munda Pahad trek (2 km) through dense forests. The trail offers beautiful views and opportunities to spot wildlife. Moderate difficulty level.

Biological Park

Visit the Chidiya Tapu Biological Park to see endemic species of birds, animals, and reptiles. Entry fee: ₹50 per person. Open: 6:00 AM - 5:00 PM.

Best Time to Visit

October to May

The best time to visit Chidiya Tapu is between October and May when the weather is pleasant and bird activity is high. For bird watching, visit early morning (6:00 AM - 9:00 AM). For sunset, arrive by 4:30 PM to secure a good viewing spot.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Chidiya Tapu for sunset?

Arrive by 4:30 PM to get a good viewing spot. Sunset usually occurs between 5:00 PM and 6:00 PM depending on the season. The sunset point can get crowded, so early arrival is recommended.

Can I spot birds at Chidiya Tapu?

Yes! Chidiya Tapu is home to 46+ bird species including endemic ones like Andaman Woodpecker, Andaman Drongo, and White-headed Starling. Visit early morning (6:00 AM - 9:00 AM) for the best bird watching experience. Carry binoculars.

Is there an entry fee for Chidiya Tapu?

The beach and sunset point are free to visit. However, the Biological Park charges ₹50 per person entry fee. Camera charges may apply for professional photography equipment.

Are there food and facilities at Chidiya Tapu?

Yes, there are small food stalls and local restaurants near the beach selling snacks, fresh coconut water, and basic meals. Washroom facilities are available. However, carry your own water and snacks for convenience.

Can I swim at Chidiya Tapu beach?

Swimming is possible at Munda Pahad Beach (2 km trek from main area). The main sunset point beach has rocky areas, so swimming is not recommended there. Always check with locals about safe swimming spots.

How much time should I spend at Chidiya Tapu?

Plan for 3-4 hours. If you're interested in bird watching, arrive early morning and spend 2-3 hours. For sunset, arrive by 4:30 PM and stay until 6:00 PM. You can also do the Munda Pahad trek which takes 1-2 hours.

Is Chidiya Tapu suitable for families with kids?

Yes, Chidiya Tapu is family-friendly. Kids will enjoy the Biological Park, beach activities, and sunset viewing. However, supervise children during trekking and near water. The area is safe and peaceful.

Can I do the Munda Pahad trek?

Yes, the Munda Pahad trek is a 2 km trail through dense forest leading to a secluded beach. The trek is of moderate difficulty and takes 30-45 minutes one way. Wear proper shoes and carry water. The beach at the end is beautiful and less crowded.
