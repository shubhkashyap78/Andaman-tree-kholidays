Elephant Beach

Snorkeling Paradise with Vibrant Coral Reefs

Havelock Island, Andaman

About Elephant Beach

Elephant Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Havelock Island, famous for its crystal-clear shallow waters and vibrant coral reefs. This beach is a paradise for water sports enthusiasts, offering activities like snorkeling, sea walking, jet skiing, and banana boat rides. The beach is accessible via a scenic 30-minute trek through dense tropical forest or a quick 20-minute boat ride from Havelock jetty. The underwater world here is teeming with colorful fish, corals, and marine life, making it a must-visit destination for adventure seekers.

Water Activities Available

Snorkeling

Explore vibrant coral reefs and colorful fish in shallow clear waters. Equipment and life jackets provided. Perfect for beginners.

Price: ₹500-800 per person

Sea Walk

Walk on the ocean floor with a special helmet. No swimming skills required. Experience marine life up close.

Price: ₹3,500-4,000 per person

Jet Ski & Banana Ride

Thrilling water sports including jet skiing, banana boat rides, and speed boat rides for adventure lovers.

Price: ₹500-1,500 per activity

How to Reach Elephant Beach

Option 1: Boat Ride (Recommended)

Duration: 20 minutes from Havelock jetty

Cost: ₹1,000-1,500 per person (round trip)

Timings: 7:00 AM to 3:00 PM (subject to weather)

Option 2: Trekking

Duration: 30-40 minutes through tropical forest

Difficulty: Easy to moderate

Cost: Free (no entry fee)

*Trek starts from Radhanagar Beach road

Best Time to Visit

October to May

The best time to visit Elephant Beach is between October and May when the sea is calm and water visibility is excellent for snorkeling. Early morning visits (7:00 AM - 10:00 AM) offer the best visibility and fewer crowds.

Avoid: Monsoon season (June to September) due to rough seas and limited water sports.

Important Tips

  • Carry sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses for sun protection
  • Wear comfortable clothes and water shoes
  • Book water activities in advance during peak season
  • Carry drinking water and light snacks
  • Keep valuables in waterproof bags
  • Follow safety instructions from instructors
  • Don't touch or damage coral reefs
  • Avoid littering - keep the beach clean
  • Check weather conditions before planning
  • Carry cash as card facilities may not be available

Frequently Asked Questions

Is snorkeling safe for beginners at Elephant Beach?

Yes, Elephant Beach is perfect for beginners. The water is shallow (3-10 feet), calm, and clear. Life jackets and snorkeling equipment are provided, and professional instructors guide you throughout the activity.

Which is better - boat ride or trekking to Elephant Beach?

Boat ride is faster (20 minutes) and more convenient, especially for families with kids or elderly. Trekking (30-40 minutes) is free and offers a scenic forest experience. Choose based on your fitness level and time availability.

What is the cost of water activities at Elephant Beach?

Snorkeling: ₹500-800, Sea Walk: ₹3,500-4,000, Jet Ski: ₹500-1,000, Banana Ride: ₹500-800. Prices may vary by season and operator. Combo packages are available at discounted rates.

Can I do sea walk if I don't know swimming?

Yes! Sea walk doesn't require swimming skills. You wear a special helmet that allows you to breathe normally underwater while walking on the ocean floor. It's safe and suitable for non-swimmers.

Are there food and facilities at Elephant Beach?

Basic food stalls are available selling snacks, fresh coconut water, and light meals. Changing rooms and washrooms are available. However, facilities are limited, so carry essentials with you.

What time should I reach Elephant Beach?

Reach by 7:00-8:00 AM for the best experience. Early morning offers excellent water visibility for snorkeling, fewer crowds, and you can enjoy multiple activities before afternoon heat.

Is underwater photography available?

Yes, underwater photography services are available at ₹500-1,000. You can also rent waterproof camera cases or bring your own GoPro to capture memories.

Can I visit Elephant Beach during monsoon?

Not recommended. During monsoon (June-September), the sea is rough, boat services are limited, and water sports are suspended for safety. Plan your visit between October and May.
