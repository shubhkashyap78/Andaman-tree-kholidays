Kalapathar Beach

Serene Beach with Black Rocks & Turquoise Waters

Havelock Island, Andaman

About Kalapathar Beach

Kalapathar Beach, named after the black rocks (Kala Pathar means black stone) scattered along the shoreline, is one of the most picturesque and serene beaches in Havelock Island. The stunning contrast of black volcanic rocks against the turquoise blue waters and white sand creates a mesmerizing landscape perfect for photography. Unlike the more crowded beaches, Kalapathar offers a peaceful and tranquil environment ideal for relaxation, sunrise viewing, and romantic walks. The beach is easily accessible by road and is perfect for those seeking solitude and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit

October to May

The best time to visit Kalapathar Beach is between October and May when the weather is pleasant and the sea is calm. For the most spectacular experience, visit during sunrise (5:30 AM - 6:30 AM) when the golden light illuminates the black rocks creating magical photo opportunities.

Best for Sunrise: 5:30 AM - 6:30 AM

Best for Photography: Early morning or late afternoon

Things to Do at Kalapathar Beach

Photography

Capture stunning photographs with the unique black rocks, turquoise waters, and white sand. Perfect for Instagram, pre-wedding shoots, and professional photography.

Beach Walks

Take peaceful walks along the shoreline, explore the black rock formations, and enjoy the serene atmosphere away from crowds.

Relaxation

Sit on the rocks, listen to the waves, read a book, or simply relax in the peaceful environment surrounded by natural beauty.

How to Reach Kalapathar Beach

From Havelock Jetty: 10 km by road (25-30 minutes)

Transport Options:

  • Auto-rickshaw: ₹300-400 (one way)
  • Taxi: ₹500-700 (one way)
  • Rental bike/scooter: ₹400-600 per day

Entry Fee: Free for all visitors

Parking: Available near the beach

Important Tips

  • Visit during sunrise for the best photography experience
  • Wear comfortable footwear for walking on rocks
  • Carry sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses
  • Bring your camera or smartphone for photos
  • Swimming is not recommended due to rocky seabed
  • Carry drinking water and snacks (limited stalls)
  • Respect the environment - don't litter
  • Be careful while climbing on rocks
  • Check tide timings before visiting
  • Combine with visit to Radhanagar Beach (nearby)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I swim at Kalapathar Beach?

Swimming is not recommended at Kalapathar Beach due to the rocky seabed and occasional strong currents. The beach is best for photography, relaxation, and wading in shallow waters.

What is the best time to visit for photography?

Early morning during sunrise (5:30 AM - 6:30 AM) is the best time for photography. The golden light creates stunning contrasts with the black rocks. Late afternoon (4:00 PM - 5:30 PM) is also good for photography.

Are there food and facilities at Kalapathar Beach?

Facilities are very limited at Kalapathar Beach. There are 1-2 small stalls selling snacks and coconut water. No proper restaurants or washrooms. Carry your own food, water, and essentials.

How much time should I spend at Kalapathar Beach?

1-2 hours is sufficient to explore the beach, take photographs, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere. You can combine it with visits to nearby Radhanagar or Vijaynagar beaches.

Is Kalapathar Beach suitable for families with kids?

Yes, but with caution. The beach is peaceful and safe for families. However, supervise children near the rocks and water. The rocky terrain may not be ideal for very young children.

Can I do a pre-wedding photoshoot at Kalapathar Beach?

Absolutely! Kalapathar Beach is one of the most popular spots for pre-wedding and professional photoshoots in Andaman. The unique black rocks and turquoise waters create stunning backdrops. No special permission required.

What is the meaning of Kalapathar?

Kalapathar means "Black Stone" in Hindi. The beach is named after the unique black volcanic rocks scattered along the shoreline, which create a striking contrast with the white sand and blue waters.

Is Kalapathar Beach crowded?

No, Kalapathar Beach is much less crowded compared to Radhanagar or Elephant Beach. It's perfect for those seeking peace, solitude, and a quiet beach experience away from tourist crowds.
