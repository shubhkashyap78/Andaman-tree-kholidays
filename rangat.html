Offbeat Destination with Mangrove Creeks & Turtle Nesting

Middle Andaman, Andaman Islands

About Rangat

Rangat is a serene and offbeat destination located in Middle Andaman, approximately 170 km from Port Blair. Known for its untouched natural beauty, Rangat offers a perfect escape from the crowded tourist spots. The region is famous for its mangrove creeks, turtle nesting beaches, limestone caves, and peaceful beaches. Rangat serves as a gateway to some of the most pristine and unexplored areas of Andaman including Amkunj Beach (turtle nesting site), Dhaninallah Mangrove Nature Walkway, Moricedera Beach, and Panchavati Hills. The town is ideal for travelers seeking tranquility, nature exploration, and authentic island experiences away from commercial tourism.

Top Attractions in Rangat

Amkunj Beach

Famous turtle nesting beach where Olive Ridley turtles come to lay eggs (December to March). Beautiful white sand beach with clear waters. Turtle nesting is a protected activity - observe from distance.

Distance: 8 km from Rangat | Entry: Free

Dhaninallah Mangrove Walkway

1.2 km wooden walkway through dense mangrove forests. Spot birds, crabs, and unique mangrove ecosystem. Best visited during low tide. Peaceful and educational experience.

Distance: 15 km from Rangat | Entry: ₹25

Moricedera Beach

Pristine and secluded beach with white sand and turquoise waters. Perfect for swimming and relaxation. Less crowded and peaceful. Ideal for picnics and beach camping.

Distance: 12 km from Rangat | Entry: Free

Panchavati Hills

Scenic viewpoint offering panoramic views of the surrounding islands and sea. Short trek to the top. Best visited during sunrise or sunset. Photography paradise.

Distance: 5 km from Rangat | Entry: Free

Best Time to Visit Rangat

October to March

The best time to visit Rangat is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities. December to March is the turtle nesting season at Amkunj Beach. Avoid monsoon season (June to September) due to heavy rainfall and limited ferry services.

Turtle Nesting Season: December to March

Best Weather: November to February

How to Reach Rangat

From Port Blair: 170 km by road (4-5 hours) or ferry (3-4 hours)

By Road:

  • Government bus: ₹200-300 per person
  • Private taxi: ₹3,500-4,500 (full vehicle)
  • Shared taxi: ₹500-700 per person

By Ferry:

  • Government ferry from Port Blair to Rangat
  • Duration: 3-4 hours | Cost: ₹300-500
  • Frequency: 2-3 times per week (check schedule)

Where to Stay in Rangat

Rangat offers budget-friendly accommodation options including government guesthouses, private hotels, and homestays. Most hotels are located in Rangat Bazaar area.

Budget Hotels: ₹800-1,500 per night

Mid-Range Hotels: ₹1,500-3,000 per night

Homestays: ₹1,000-2,000 per night

*Advance booking recommended during peak season

Important Tips for Visiting Rangat

  • Carry sufficient cash - limited ATMs and card facilities
  • Book accommodation in advance during peak season
  • Hire local guide for exploring offbeat locations
  • Carry mosquito repellent and basic medicines
  • Respect turtle nesting sites - maintain distance
  • Check ferry schedules in advance if traveling by sea
  • Carry food and water for day trips to beaches
  • Mobile network may be limited in some areas
  • Wear comfortable shoes for trekking and walking
  • Respect local culture and environment

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Rangat worth visiting?

Yes! Rangat is perfect for travelers seeking offbeat experiences, nature exploration, and peaceful beaches away from tourist crowds. It's ideal for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those interested in turtle nesting and mangrove ecosystems.

When can I see turtle nesting at Amkunj Beach?

Olive Ridley turtles nest at Amkunj Beach between December and March. The best time to witness nesting is during night hours (after sunset). However, maintain distance and don't disturb the turtles as it's a protected activity.

How many days should I spend in Rangat?

2-3 days are sufficient to explore Rangat's main attractions including Amkunj Beach, Dhaninallah Mangrove Walkway, Moricedera Beach, and Panchavati Hills. You can also use Rangat as a stopover while traveling to Diglipur.

What is the Dhaninallah Mangrove Walkway?

It's a 1.2 km wooden walkway through dense mangrove forests. You can walk through the mangroves, spot birds, crabs, and learn about the unique ecosystem. Entry fee is ₹25 per person. Best visited during low tide.

Are there good hotels in Rangat?

Rangat has budget-friendly hotels, government guesthouses, and homestays. While not as luxurious as Port Blair or Havelock, the accommodation is clean and comfortable. Prices range from ₹800-3,000 per night. Book in advance during peak season.

How do I reach Rangat from Port Blair?

You can reach Rangat by road (170 km, 4-5 hours) via government bus, private taxi, or shared taxi. Alternatively, take a government ferry (3-4 hours, 2-3 times per week). Road journey is more convenient and frequent.

Is Rangat safe for solo travelers?

Yes, Rangat is safe for solo travelers. The local community is friendly and helpful. However, as it's an offbeat destination, inform someone about your travel plans, carry sufficient cash, and hire local guides for exploring remote areas.

What should I carry when visiting Rangat?

Carry sufficient cash (limited ATMs), mosquito repellent, sunscreen, comfortable shoes, basic medicines, water bottles, and snacks. Also carry power bank as electricity may be limited in some areas. Mobile network can be patchy.
