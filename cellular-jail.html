Cellular Jail National Memorial

Witness India's Freedom Struggle History

Port Blair, Andaman

About Cellular Jail

Cellular Jail, also known as Kala Pani, is a colonial prison that stands as a testimony to the sacrifices made by Indian freedom fighters. Built by the British in 1906, this jail was used to exile political prisoners during the struggle for India's independence. The jail originally had seven wings radiating from a central tower, but only three wings remain today. The famous Light and Sound Show narrates the heroic saga of the freedom fighters who were imprisoned here.

Historical Significance

  • Built in 1906 by the British colonial government
  • Housed political prisoners during India's freedom struggle
  • Originally had 698 cells designed for solitary confinement
  • Famous freedom fighters like Veer Savarkar were imprisoned here
  • Declared a National Memorial in 1979

  • Gallows where freedom fighters were hanged
  • Symbol of India's struggle for independence
  • Must-visit for history enthusiasts and patriots

Light and Sound Show

The Light and Sound Show is a must-watch experience that brings alive the history of Cellular Jail and the sacrifices of freedom fighters. The 50-minute show uses dramatic lighting, sound effects, and narration to tell the story of India's struggle for independence. The show is conducted in both Hindi and English.

Duration: 50 minutes

Languages: Hindi (5:15 PM) & English (6:15 PM)

Booking: Advance booking recommended during peak season

What to See

Solitary Cells

Visit the tiny 4.5 x 2.7 meter cells where prisoners were kept in solitary confinement with minimal light and ventilation.

Gallows

See the gallows where freedom fighters were hanged, a somber reminder of their ultimate sacrifice.

Photo Gallery

Explore the museum with photographs, documents, and artifacts from the colonial era and freedom struggle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is photography allowed inside Cellular Jail?

Yes, photography is allowed in most areas of the jail. However, photography is not permitted during the Light and Sound Show.

How much time is needed to visit Cellular Jail?

Plan for 2-3 hours including the museum visit and Light and Sound Show. The museum takes about 1 hour, and the show is 50 minutes long.

Should I book Light and Sound Show tickets in advance?

Yes, it's recommended to book tickets in advance during peak season (December to February) as seats fill up quickly. Tickets can be booked online or at the counter.

Is there a dress code for visiting Cellular Jail?

There is no strict dress code, but visitors are expected to dress modestly and respectfully as it is a national memorial. Avoid wearing shorts or revealing clothing.

Can I visit Cellular Jail on Monday?

No, the museum is closed on Mondays. However, the Light and Sound Show runs every day including Mondays. Plan your visit accordingly.
