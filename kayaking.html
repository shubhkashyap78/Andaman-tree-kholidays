What to Expect

Check-in & Registration: Meet at the center, complete registration and sign waiver forms. Our team will welcome you and answer any questions.

Safety Briefing: Comprehensive briefing on paddling techniques, emergency procedures, and equipment fitting. All gear is inspected and provided.

Launch into Waters: Paddle out into peaceful tidal creeks toward the mangrove forests. Your guide leads the way under the open night sky.

Bioluminescence Zone: Reach the glowing waters — stir the surface and watch plankton light up in vivid blues and greens. A once-in-a-lifetime sight.

Return & Wrap Up: Gently return to shore, share experiences, and receive your photographs. Total experience lasts approximately 1.5 hours.

Kayaking Locations

North Bay: The primary launch point for mangrove kayaking. Paddle through calm creeks surrounded by dense forests — ideal for spotting marine life and rich biodiversity.

Corbyn's Cove Beach: A scenic route through lagoons and mangroves. Professionals provide safety briefings and equipment before every session — great for a coastal paddle experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What makes night kayaking in Port Blair unique?
    Night kayaking is unique because of the bioluminescent plankton in the water. As you paddle, the water glows electric blue-green — a rare natural phenomenon.
  2. Is it safe for beginners and non-swimmers?
    Yes! This experience is designed for all levels. However, basic swimming skills are recommended. All participants receive safety briefings, life jackets, and professional guidance.
  3. What is the best time to do night kayaking?
    Evening/night sessions showcase the bioluminescence best. October to May is ideal for calm waters. The phenomenon is most visible on moonless nights.
  4. Is bioluminescence always visible?
    Bioluminescence depends on natural plankton activity and is generally visible year-round in Andaman. Visibility is highest on clear, dark nights.
  5. What should I wear and bring?
    Wear light, comfortable clothing that can get wet. Bring a change of clothes, water shoes or flip-flops, and a waterproof bag for your phone/camera.
